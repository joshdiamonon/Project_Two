# title

Twenty Something

# project overview

To me, community starts with learning to forgive, learning to reconnect, and learning to rebuild. Growing up, I never felt like I had a community or village to fall back to like I saw many of my peers and friends did. I was never close to my family, especially my parents even though both were present in my life the entire time. I grew up the youngest with a significant age gap between my siblings and I, which led me to feeling very neglected as it felt as my parents didn't really raise me. Later coming out as gay to my family, which really set a layers to our already complicated relationship, put a strain on that connection. As Asian Americans, family is almost always the center of our culture, which is why not having that growing up, I felt very disconnected. With spacee and time, I learned more about myself and my upbringing, leading me to understand and forgive my parents. The artist, Luke Chiang and his work perfectly encapsulates the Asian American experience many, including myself, have faced. His work I'm presenting brings to light the acknowledgement of the challenges our parents sacraficed to give us a better life that as we didn't really understand our parents as kids. Understanding that our parents were twenty something year olds trying to figure out life at one point, just as we stand here today, twenty something, figuring out life just as they did.
